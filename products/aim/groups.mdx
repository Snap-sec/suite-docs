## Overview

Groups in **Asset Intelligence Management (AIM)** allow you to cluster similar assets together ‚Äî such as *DNS-active subdomains*, *Cloudflare-reported assets*, or *API endpoints*.  
This enables teams to manage related resources collectively, monitor them for changes, and apply consistent analysis or reporting.

Groups can be static (manually created) or dynamic (auto-populated based on tags and rules).

## Creating a New Group

You can create a group by defining its name, selecting tags, and providing a clear description of what the group represents.

<Frame caption="Create Group Dialog">
  <img style={{ borderRadius: '0.5rem' }} src="/images/aim/groups1.png" />
</Frame>

### Steps to Create a Group

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click on Groups in Sidebar" icon="rectangle-list">
    Navigate to **Groups** under AIM‚Äôs left navigation menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click + Create" icon="plus">
    A modal will appear to define the new group.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Name, Tags, and Description" icon="pen-to-square">
    For example, *All DNS Active Subdomains* can include assets tagged with `A` and `DNS`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save Group" icon="circle-check">
    The new group will appear in the Groups list with its asset count.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Viewing Group Assets

Clicking on any group opens a filtered view in the **Global Catalog**, showing only assets belonging to that group.  
You can see complete details ‚Äî source, vulnerabilities, owners, risk scores, and tags.

## Tips

- Use clear naming conventions (e.g., `Production APIs`, `Decommissioned IPs`, `Active DNS Hosts`).  
- Leverage **tags** to auto-populate dynamic groups.  
- Regularly review group membership to maintain clean inventory boundaries.  
- Share group-based links across modules like VM and ASM to maintain data consistency.
