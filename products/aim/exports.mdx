## Overview

The **Export** feature in Asset Intelligence Management (AIM) allows you to extract your entire asset inventory or specific asset categories into a downloadable file format like CSV.  
Exports help with offline audits, data sharing, and integration with BI or reporting tools.

## Export Options

AIM offers two modes of export:

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="Export Full Catalogue" icon="cloud-arrow-down">
    Download a complete export of all assets across categories — ideal for backups, offline analysis, and compliance archives.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Export Specific Assets" icon="filter">
    Choose an asset type (e.g., API, DNS, IP, Repository) and format to export only the relevant dataset.
  </Card>
</Columns>

---

## How to Export Data

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Export Panel" icon="folder-open">
    From the sidebar, navigate to **Export** to open the export configuration window.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Export Mode" icon="list-check">
    Click **Export Full Catalogue** to download the entire inventory, or proceed to select asset types manually.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Asset Type" icon="network-wired">
    Use the dropdown to pick specific categories like *API, DNS, Employee, IP,* or *Certificate.*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Format" icon="file-csv">
    Currently, **CSV** is supported, making it easy to import into Excel, Google Sheets, or Power BI.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export File" icon="download">
    Click **Export Selected Asset** to generate and download the file.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Output Example

After export, you’ll receive a CSV file containing detailed asset information, including:

- Asset name or domain  
- Associated risk score  
- Number of vulnerabilities  
- Tags and source adapter  
- Discovery timestamps  

<Frame caption="Exported CSV Data Example">
  <img style={{ borderRadius: '0.5rem' }} src="/images/aim/export3.png" />
</Frame>

> Example columns:
> - **Subdomain** – Asset identifier  
> - **NoOfVuln** – Vulnerability count  
> - **Risk** – Auto-calculated risk score  
> - **Found On** – Date and time of asset discovery

## Best Practices

- Regularly export your full catalog for archival and compliance reviews.  
- Combine exports with filters in the Global Catalog for focused reports.  
- Use spreadsheet tools to create pivot tables, track trends, and share progress with teams.
