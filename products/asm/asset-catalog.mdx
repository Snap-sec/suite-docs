## Overview

The **Asset Catalog** is the foundation of **Attack Surface Management (ASM)** — providing a **live, continuously updated inventory** of everything exposed across your organization’s external footprint.

Instead of fragmented visibility across tools, ASM consolidates all discovered assets into a **single, structured system**, where every entity is enriched, categorized, and correlated in real time.

From subdomains and IPs to technologies and DNS records, the catalog ensures that **nothing exposed remains unknown**.

## How It Works

ASM continuously performs **active and passive discovery** across:

- Domains and subdomains  
- IP ranges and infrastructure  
- Certificate transparency logs  
- DNS records and internet-wide datasets  

Every discovered asset is:

- **Normalized** into a structured format  
- **Enriched** with metadata (ownership, exposure, tech stack)  
- **Linked** to related entities (IP ↔ domain ↔ port ↔ technology)  
- **Continuously updated** as changes occur  

This creates a **living map of your attack surface**, not just a static inventory.

## Asset Views

The catalog is broken down into dedicated views — each designed to give deep visibility into a specific layer of your attack surface.

---

### Subdomains

Subdomains represent the **entry points of your external surface**.

ASM discovers subdomains using a combination of DNS enumeration, passive intelligence, and certificate data — ensuring even hidden or forgotten assets are surfaced.

<Frame caption="Subdomain inventory with ports, IP mappings, WAF detection, and exposure status">
  <img src="/images/asm/subdomains.png" alt="ASM Subdomains View" />
</Frame>

**What You Get:**
- Live vs inactive subdomain tracking  
- Associated **IP addresses and open ports**  
- WAF detection (e.g., Cloudflare, DenyAll)  
- External vs internal exposure classification  
- Signal-based risk indicators  

**Why It Matters:**
Subdomains are often the weakest link — forgotten apps, staging environments, or misconfigured DNS entries can become attack vectors.

---

### Web Servers

The Web Servers view focuses on **HTTP/HTTPS-exposed services** across your infrastructure.

ASM fingerprints each service to identify behavior, technologies, and response characteristics.

<Frame caption="Web servers with detected technologies, ports, status codes, and exposure context">
  <img src="/images/asm/webservers.png" alt="ASM Web Servers View" />
</Frame>

**What You Get:**
- HTTP status validation (200, 404, etc.)  
- Detected technologies and frameworks  
- Open ports and exposed services  
- External exposure classification  
- Risk indicators per service  

**Why It Matters:**
This is where real attack surfaces live — APIs, dashboards, admin panels, and applications.

---

### IP Addresses

The IP view provides a **network-level perspective** of your attack surface.

Every discovered IP is enriched with location, hosting provider, and associated assets.

<Frame caption="IP inventory with geolocation, ASN, open ports, and asset mapping">
  <img src="/images/asm/ips.png" alt="ASM IP Addresses View" />
</Frame>

**What You Get:**
- IP ↔ domain mapping  
- Geolocation and hosting provider (ASN)  
- Open ports per IP  
- External exposure tracking  
- Detection timestamps  

**Why It Matters:**
Understanding infrastructure ownership is critical for identifying **shadow assets, cloud misconfigurations, and unmanaged hosts**.

---

### Certificates

The Certificates view gives full visibility into your **SSL/TLS posture**.

ASM continuously tracks certificates across domains and IPs.

<Frame caption="Certificates with validity status, issuers, and expiration tracking">
  <img src="/images/asm/certificates.png" alt="ASM Certificates View" />
</Frame>

**What You Get:**
- Valid, expired, and expiring certificates  
- Issuer and certificate metadata  
- Domain associations  
- Risk signals tied to certificate issues  

**Why It Matters:**
Expired or misconfigured certificates can cause outages — and also indicate neglected assets.

---

### DNS Records

DNS is the backbone of your attack surface — and often the source of hidden exposure.

ASM tracks all DNS records and monitors changes over time.

<Frame caption="DNS records across A, CNAME, MX, TXT and other types">
  <img src="/images/asm/dns-records.png" alt="ASM DNS Records View" />
</Frame>

**What You Get:**
- Full DNS record inventory (A, CNAME, MX, TXT, etc.)  
- Mapping between domains and infrastructure  
- Detection timestamps and updates  
- Signal tracking for anomalies  

**Why It Matters:**
Misconfigured or dangling DNS records can expose infrastructure you no longer control.

---

### Ports

The Ports view highlights **network-level exposure** across all assets.

ASM scans and tracks open ports, services, and versions.

<Frame caption="Open ports with services, versions, and exposure status">
  <img src="/images/asm/ports.png" alt="ASM Ports View" />
</Frame>

**What You Get:**
- Open ports and associated services  
- Service versions (e.g., nginx, OpenSSH)  
- Exposure status (external/internal)  
- Detection timestamps  

**Why It Matters:**
Unexpected open ports are one of the most common entry points for attackers.

---

### Technologies

The Technologies view provides a **high-level inventory of all detected technologies** across your attack surface.

Instead of raw data, technologies are grouped into structured categories — making it easy to understand your stack at scale.

<Frame caption="Technology catalog showing frameworks, platforms, and services detected across assets">
  <img src="/images/asm/technologies.png" alt="ASM Technologies View" />
</Frame>

**What You Get:**
- Technologies like **Nginx, Node.js, React, Next.js, Cloudflare, Netlify**  
- Categorization (CDN, Web Servers, Analytics, Frameworks, etc.)  
- Asset-level mapping for each technology  
- Clean card-based visualization for exploration  

**Why It Matters:**
- Identify outdated or vulnerable tech stacks  
- Understand platform usage across teams  
- Detect unauthorized or unexpected technologies  
- Support vulnerability prioritization

## Correlation Across Assets

What makes the Asset Catalog powerful is not just visibility — but **correlation**.

Every asset is connected:

- Subdomain → IP → Ports → Technologies → Certificate  
- DNS → Infrastructure → Exposure → Signals  

This creates a **context-rich graph of your attack surface**, enabling:

- Faster root cause analysis  
- Better risk prioritization  
- Smarter automation across security workflows  

---

## Continuous Intelligence

The catalog is not static — it is **continuously refreshed**.

ASM integrates with multiple data sources and scanners to:

- Discover new assets in real time  
- Detect changes instantly  
- Enrich existing data automatically  

This ensures your inventory always reflects **your current attack surface — not yesterday’s**.
