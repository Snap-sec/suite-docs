## Business Units Overview

The **Business Units** dashboard provides a high-level view of all organizational units and their associated security posture.  
Each card represents a distinct business function, helping teams track ownership, vulnerability exposure, and assessment coverage.

<Frame caption="Business Units Overview">
  <img src="/images/aim/business-units-overview.png" />
</Frame>

### What You Can See
- Business unit name and description  
- Assigned owner  
- Severity counters (Critical → Info)  
- Quick access to detailed unit insights  

This view enables leadership and security teams to quickly understand risk distribution across departments.

---

## Create a Business Unit

New business units can be created to align assets and assessments with organizational structure.

<Frame caption="Create Business Unit">
  <img src="/images/aim/business-units-create.png" />
</Frame>

### Fields Required
- **Name** — Business unit identifier (e.g., Security Operations, DevOps)
- **Description** — Purpose and responsibility of the unit
- **Owner** — Accountable person or team
- **Logo URL** *(optional)* — Visual identifier for the unit

Once created, the business unit becomes available for asset and assessment association.

## Business Unit Details (Inside a Business Unit)

Clicking **View Details** opens the dedicated business unit page with summarized security metrics.

<Frame caption="Business Unit Summary View">
  <img src="/images/aim/business-units-details.png" />
</Frame>

### Summary Metrics Include
- **Total Vulnerabilities**
- **Total Assessments**
- **Open Vulnerabilities**

- **Average CVSS Score**

Ownership information is clearly displayed to ensure accountability and streamlined remediation workflows.

## Security Analytics & Associated Assessments

Scrolling further within a business unit reveals deeper analytics and assessment relationships.

<Frame caption="Business Unit Analytics & Assessments">
  <img src="/images/aim/business-units-analytics.png" />
</Frame>

### Available Insights
- **Vulnerability Timeline** — Historical view of detections over time
- **Severity Distribution** — Breakdown by Critical, High, Medium, Low
- **Detections vs Resolutions** — Measure remediation effectiveness
- **Vulnerabilities by Status** — Track lifecycle states (In Progress, Fixed, Resolved, etc.)
- **Associated Assessments** — Linked security assessments assigned to the unit

This section helps teams understand not just *what* vulnerabilities exist, but *how effectively* they are being addressed.
