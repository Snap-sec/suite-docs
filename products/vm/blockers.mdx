## Overview

**Blockers** help teams track issues that delay or prevent vulnerability remediation.

Whenever progress is blocked — due to dependencies, missing access, or validation delays — a blocker ensures the issue is visible, assigned, and tracked until resolution.

Each blocker is linked to a vulnerability and includes ownership, status, and activity history.

<Callout icon="shield-check" color="green">
Blockers ensure that no remediation delay goes unnoticed — every dependency or issue is tracked with clear ownership.
</Callout>

---

## What You Can Do

- **Track All Blockers**
  - View all blockers across vulnerabilities  

- **Filter and Search**
  - Quickly find blockers using status, priority, or assignee  

- **Manage Ownership**
  - Assign blockers to responsible team members  

- **Monitor Status**
  - Track blockers as *Pending* or *Resolved*

## Creating a Blocker

Blockers are created directly from within a vulnerability when progress is blocked.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Vulnerability">
    Navigate to a vulnerability where progress is stalled.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use Comment Panel">
    In the **Comment & Blocker Panel**, enter details and enable **Create a Blocker**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit Blocker">
    Add description and assign a user, then submit the blocker.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing Blockers

Once created, blockers can be updated and resolved directly.

- **Mark as Resolved**
  - Close the blocker once the issue is fixed  

- **Reopen Blocker**
  - Reopen if the issue persists  

- **Track Ownership**
  - View assigned user and creator
